⛸️ Skaters

Conservation of linear momentum and the link between force, mass and acceleration.

The idea

Two skaters push each other. The force is identical on both (3rd law), but since a = F/m, the lighter one moves away faster. In an isolated system (no friction), the total momentum is conserved and stays zero: the two momenta are equal and opposite. With friction (an external force), the total momentum stops being conserved and both eventually stop.

The formulas

FA→B = − FB→A   ⇒   mA·vA + mB·vB = 0 Same force and same time ⇒ opposite impulses ⇒ opposite momenta that sum to zero.
vA / vB = mB / mA The speeds end up in the inverse ratio of the masses: the light one shoots off, the heavy one barely moves.
Impulse: J = F·Δt = Δp The same force over the same time gives each one the same magnitude of momentum.

Worked example

A 300 N push for 0.4 s between blue (60 kg) and red (90 kg):

Impulse = F·Δt = 300·0.4 = 120 N·s on each one.

vblue = 120/60 = 2.0 m/s; vred = 120/90 = 1.33 m/s.

Total momentum = 60·2.0 − 90·1.33 = 120 − 120 = 0 ✓ (conserved, in vacuum/ice without friction).

In the simulator, on ice/vacuum the total momentum stays zero "forever"; on asphalt the friction (an external force) slows both and the total stops being zero - a great chance to discuss what an isolated system is.
