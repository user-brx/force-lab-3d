🎯 .50 BMG Rifle

Conservation of momentum (recoil), external ballistics and penetration of barriers.

The idea

The powder gas pushes the bullet forward and the back of the barrel backward with the same force (3rd law): bullet and gun gain equal and opposite momenta that sum to zero. The muzzle brake deflects part of the gas sideways, cutting the felt recoil - without violating conservation (the total impulse of bullet + gas + gun is still zero).

The formulas

pbullet = mb·vb = mgun·vrecoil Conservation of momentum: the gun's recoil balances the bullet's momentum.
Ek = ½·m·v²   |   Fdrag = ½·ρ·v²·Cd(Mach)·A The bullet's energy is much larger than the gun's (same p, but v² favors the light one). Cd varies with Mach number.
Bullet drop: Δy = ½·g·t² A horizontal shot falls like any object: from 0.5 m it reaches the ground in t=√(2·0.5/g)≈0.32 s - independent of caliber.
Penetration (Poncelet): P = m / (2·A·ρtarget) · ln( 1 + ρtarget·v² / R ) R = dynamic strength of the material. Exit speed through thickness T: vr² = (v²+R/ρ)·e^(−2Aρ T/m) − R/ρ.

Worked example

Bullet momentum: p = 0.042·890 = 37.4 kg·m/s.

Recoil without brake: v = p/mgun = 37.4/14 = 2.67 m/s; with a muzzle brake (~38 %) ≈ 1.0 m/s.

Energies: bullet ½·0.042·890² ≈ 16.6 kJ; gun ½·14·2.67² ≈ 50 J - the bullet carries ~300× more energy even though the momentum is the same (because E ∝ v²).
