✈️ Airplane

Lift, induced and parasitic drag, and the sound barrier (wave drag).

The idea

The propeller/turbine throws air backward and the air pushes the plane forward (3rd law). The wings deflect air downward (downwash) and the air pushes the wings up: that's lift. Everything depends on the air - without an atmosphere the plane does not fly (a direct contrast with the rocket). Near Mach 1 the wave drag appears, the famous "sound barrier".

The formulas

L = ½ · ρ · v² · S · CL    D = ½ · ρ · v² · S · CD L = lift, D = drag; S = wing area; CL, CD = coefficients.
CD = CD0 + k·CL² + CD,wave(Mach) Parasitic (CD0) + induced (∝ CL²) + wave drag (spikes near Mach 1).
vstall = √( 2·m·g / (ρ·S·CL,max) ) Minimum speed for lift to equal weight. Since v ∝ 1/√ρ, in thin air (Mars) it needs far more speed.
Mach = v / a(h)     a(h) = a₀·√( T(h)/T₀ ) The speed of sound drops with temperature (ISA lapse rate up to the tropopause).

Worked example

Stall speed (3000 kg on Earth): vstall = √(2·3000·9.81 / (1.225·30·1.5)) = √(58,860 / 55.1) ≈ 32.7 m/s (~118 km/h).

On Mars (ρ = 0.02 kg/m³) the same plane would need v ∝ 1/√ρ ≈ 7.8× more speed - practically impossible with normal wings. In the dense air of Venus (ρ = 65) it's the opposite: it flies almost stationary.
