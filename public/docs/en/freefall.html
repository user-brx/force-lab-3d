☄️ Free Fall / Kinetic energy

Potential → kinetic energy conversion, terminal velocity and impact energy (up to meteors).

The idea

An object dropped from a height starts with gravitational potential energy and converts it into kinetic energy as it falls. With air, part becomes heat (drag) and there is a terminal velocity. On impact, the kinetic energy is released all at once - that's how a meteor digs a crater. The simulator lets you choose height (up to space), mass, initial velocity and the shape (which changes drag).

The formulas

Epot = m·g·h   ⟶   Ekin = ½·m·v² Without air: ½·m·v² = m·g·h ⇒ v = √(2·g·h), independent of mass.
vterminal = √( 2·m·g / (ρ·Cd·A) ) When drag equals weight, the fall stops accelerating. Large objects (meteors) barely slow down.
Crater (Collins 2005): Dtr = 1.161·(ρi/ρt)1/3·L0.78·v0.44·g−0.22 L = impactor diameter; Dfinal ≈ 1.25·Dtr; depth ≈ D/5 (simple crater).
Destruction radius: R = Z · (kg of TNT)1/3 Cube-root (Hopkinson-Cranz) scaling: Z≈9.5 m/kg1/3 for severe destruction (~5 psi); ≈35 for windows (~1 psi).
Equivalence: 1 kg of TNT = 4.184×10⁶ J  |  Hiroshima ≈ 6.3×10¹³ J Lets you express the impact energy in tons of TNT or Hiroshima bombs.

Worked example

10 kg rock, 50 m, no air (conceptually): v = √(2·9.81·50) = 31.3 m/s; Ekin = m·g·h = 10·9.81·50 = 4905 J ≈ 1.2 kg of TNT.

A 500 t "asteroid" at 15 km/s: E = ½·500000·15000² ≈ 5.6×10¹³ J ≈ 13 kton of TNT ≈ 0.9 Hiroshima. Crater ≈ 280 m across, severe destruction within ~2 km, windows shattered out to ~8 km.
