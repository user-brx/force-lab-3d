🚗 Car

Traction by friction, power curve (P = F·v) and aerodynamic drag.

The idea

The engine doesn't push the car directly: it spins the wheel, which pushes the ground backward by friction; by the 3rd law the ground pushes the car forward. The traction force has two limits: the engine power (at high speed) and the available friction (at launch / on ice). If the requested force exceeds the friction, the wheel spins.

The formulas

P = F · v  ⇒  Fengine = P / v With fixed power P, traction force drops as speed rises (so acceleration fades).
Ftraction = min( P/v , μs·N )   (else it spins: μk·N) At launch P/v is huge, so friction is the limit. At high speed, power is the limit.
Fdrag = ½ · ρ · Cd · A · v² ρ = air density; Cd = drag coefficient; A = frontal area. Grows with the square of speed.
vmax = ∛( 2·P / (ρ·Cd·A) ) Top speed: when the available traction equals drag.

Worked example

Drag at 100 km/h (27.8 m/s): F = ½·1.225·0.30·2.2·27.8² ≈ 312 N.

Top speed (150 kW): vmax = ∛(2·150000 / (1.225·0.30·2.2)) ≈ ∛(371,000) ≈ 72 m/s ≈ 259 km/h.

On ice (1200 kg): traction caps at μk·N = 0.03·1200·9.81 ≈ 353 N - the wheel spins without pushing.
